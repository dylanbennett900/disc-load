Health & Fitness Disclaimer

Effective 20 April 2026

⚠︎ Read this before you use Frisbee Track

Frisbee Track is an educational and training-load monitoring tool, not medical advice. It is general information designed for healthy adult athletes playing Ultimate Frisbee. It cannot account for your individual medical history, injuries, or circumstances.

Before starting any training program suggested by this app, consult a qualified medical practitioner — especially if you have any underlying condition, are returning from injury, or have not trained at intensity for an extended period.

If you feel pain, dizziness, chest discomfort, shortness of breath, or any unusual symptom while training — stop immediately and seek medical attention.

1. What Frisbee Track is — and is not

It is: a tool to help Ultimate Frisbee players estimate their training load against sport-specific benchmarks, plan weekly targets, and periodise their training around tournaments. It reflects general sport science principles and the professional judgement of Dylan Bennett as a strength and conditioning coach and sport scientist.

It is not:

  • Medical advice or a substitute for consultation with a doctor, physiotherapist, or allied-health professional
  • Personalised coaching advice
  • A diagnostic or rehabilitation tool
  • A substitute for in-person supervision from a qualified coach, especially for advanced movements
  • Appropriate for under-16s, pregnant or postpartum women without separate medical clearance, or anyone with a known cardiovascular, musculoskeletal, or metabolic condition that could be affected by high-intensity running or strength training

2. AI-generated prescriptions

The "AI Prescription" feature generates training suggestions using a large language model (currently Google Gemini) constrained by sport-science rules. These outputs:

  • Are suggestions only, based on the data you have entered
  • May be incorrect, incomplete, or unsuitable for your situation
  • Do not know your full medical or injury history
  • Should be treated with the same scepticism you would treat any general training advice

Apply your own judgement. If a prescription doesn't feel right for your body, your training age, or your current state, don't do it. When in doubt, speak with your coach, physiotherapist, or GP.

3. Risk acknowledgement

Physical training — including running, sprinting, change of direction, plyometrics, and resistance training — carries inherent risks of injury, illness, or (in very rare cases) serious harm. By using Frisbee Track and acting on information it provides, you acknowledge and accept these risks.

You confirm that:

  • You are medically cleared to undertake vigorous physical activity
  • You will stop and seek medical advice if you experience pain or unusual symptoms
  • You will not exceed what is safe for your current fitness, experience, and recovery state
  • You will warm up adequately and respect recovery time between sessions
  • You will use appropriate equipment and training environments
  • You train at your own risk

4. Limitations of the app's data

Frisbee Track's targets are calculated from GPS data of elite Ultimate Frisbee players. If you are not at that level, these targets may be too demanding. Work up to them gradually. The app does not automatically scale down its targets for recreational players — that judgement is yours.

Drill diagrams and conditioning sessions show recommended structures. They assume you have the baseline fitness and technique to perform them safely. If a movement is unfamiliar, seek qualified in-person coaching before attempting it at intensity.

5. When to see a professional, not an app

See a qualified GP, sports physician, physiotherapist, or accredited exercise physiologist if you:

  • Are recovering from a recent injury or surgery
  • Have persistent pain, swelling, or joint instability
  • Are returning to sport after a long layoff
  • Are pregnant, postpartum, or trying to conceive
  • Have a heart condition, high blood pressure, diabetes, asthma, or any chronic condition
  • Are taking medication that could affect your response to exercise
  • Are over 45 and have not trained regularly
